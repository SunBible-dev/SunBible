New Intro/Note

Like I said before, in this book I want to help you get a lot from the sermon on the mount. Probably the newer notes by me, that you'll find in color boxes, will be a bit more boring but hopefully a bit more educational than the things I wrote four years ago. Now let's get to some boring educational facts...

Our focus verses for today, the first two verses of Matthew 5 say: "And seeing the multitudes, he went up into a mountain: and when he was set, his disciples came unto him: And he opened his mouth, and taught them, saying." We can't be certain that Jesus taught this sermon on the mountain. The gospel according to Matthew is the only gospel that contains the sermon on the mount. In the gospel of Luke, you can find similar teachings to what we find in the sermon on the mount. For example, you can find the beatitudes in the Gospel of Luke in what is called the sermon on the plain. But you cannot find the sermon on the mount in any other gospel. Some of what is written in Matthew 5 through 7 may be made up by the person who wrote the book. But we can be pretty certain that what we find in the Sermon on the Mount(Matthew 5-7) is in line with the teachings of Jesus and it's worth our time studying.

The Law

Original Title: "God's Law"

Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil. 18 For verily I say unto you, Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the law, till all be fulfilled. 19 Whosoever therefore shall break one of these least commandments, and shall teach men so, he shall be called the least in the kingdom of heaven: but whosoever shall do and teach them, the same shall be called great in the kingdom of heaven. 20 For I say unto you, That except your righteousness shall exceed the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter into the kingdom of heaven. - Matthew 5:17-20

More Than The Law - Part 1: Thou Shalt Not Kill

Original title: "More Than the Law".

For I say unto you, That except your righteousness shall exceed the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter into the kingdom of heaven. 21 Ye have heard that it was said of them of old time, Thou shalt not kill; and whosoever shall kill shall be in danger of the judgment: 22 But I say unto you, That whosoever is angry with his brother without a cause shall be in danger of the judgment: and whosoever shall say to his brother, Raca, shall be in danger of the council: but whosoever shall say, Thou fool, shall be in danger of hell fire. 23 Therefore if thou bring thy gift to the altar, and there rememberest that thy brother hath ought against thee; 24 Leave there thy gift before the altar, and go thy way; first be reconciled to thy brother, and then come and offer thy gift. 25 Agree with thine adversary quickly, whiles thou art in the way with him; lest at any time the adversary deliver thee to the judge, and the judge deliver thee to the officer, and thou be cast into prison. 26 Verily I say unto thee, Thou shalt by no means come out thence, till thou hast paid the uttermost farthing. - Matthew 5:20-26

Sure there is laws and everything, but isn't there much more to Christianity than that?
This morning while I was meditating on and reading today's Bible reading, it stood out to me that to be a Christian you have to do much more than to just obey a bunch of laws. Jesus said that except your righteousness exceeds the righteousness of the Scribes and Pharisees you shall not even be able to go to heaven. The Scribes and the Pharisees were good people, they obeyed all the laws. Yet Jesus said you need to be better than them. In the old testament it says thou shalt not kill, but Jesus said that we should not even get angry at our brothers without cause or say to others, "thou fool". Thou shalt not kill is a good law, but good laws do not make good people. The Scribes, even though they did not kill, were not good people. Their heart was not right and they had no relationship with God. Christianity is about giving your heart to God. When you do that, you automatically start being good, loving, and kind. You do what's in the law plus much more.

Today's Bible reading also talks about getting right with your brother. There are a couple reasons for this, one is because God does not like sin. Another because sin has consequences, our God is a loving God, He does not like to see people thrown into prison. If you do not get right with your brother though, you will be judged, because our God is also a just God.

When we become Christians we are no longer under the law, but above it doing what's in the law automatically plus much more.
- adapted from Galatians 3:25

More Than The Law - Part 2: Thou Shalt Not Commit Adultery

Original title: "God Allows But You Must Choose"

Ye have heard that it was said by them of old time, Thou shalt not commit adultery: 28 But I say unto you, That whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart. 29 And if thy right eye offend thee, pluck it out, and cast it from thee: for it is profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into hell. 30 And if thy right hand offend thee, cut it off, and cast it from thee: for it is profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into hell. 31 It hath been said, Whosoever shall put away his wife, let him give her a writing of divorcement: 32 But I say unto you, That whosoever shall put away his wife, saving for the cause of fornication, causeth her to commit adultery: and whosoever shall marry her that is divorced committeth adultery. - Matthew 5:27-32

Today's Bible reading talks about divorce. God is totally against divorce, but He allows you to live your life the way you want. Jesus talks about divorce in another place, and he said that Moses allowed it out of the hardness of your hearts. Our God is a God of love. He does not force you to believe in Him. He does not force you to obey Him. He does not force you to love Him, He just loves you. He makes laws to protect the people that He loves. He allows you to love Him and to obey him, or to dislike Him and disobey Him. Our God is a loving God; He gives you helpful advice so that your whole body won't be cast into hell. Jesus said that you should pluck your eye out, or cut your hand off if they are causing you to sin. If there is anything in your life that is causing you to sin you should get rid of it. Anything: household appliances, pictures, toys, books, food, friends, clothes, you name it get rid of it. You do not have to get rid of everything; you could learn to use self-control. The main thing though is to try and eliminate as much temptation as possible.

He saith unto them, Moses because of the hardness of your hearts suffered you to put away your wives: but from the beginning it was not so.
- Matthew 19:8

More Than The Law - Part 3: Thou shalt not forswear thyself

Original title: "No Oaths or Swearing".

Again, ye have heard that it hath been said by them of old time, Thou shalt not forswear thyself, but shalt perform unto the Lord thine oaths: 34 But I say unto you, Swear not at all; neither by heaven; for it is God's throne: 35 Nor by the earth; for it is his footstool: neither by Jerusalem; for it is the city of the great King. 36 Neither shalt thou swear by thy head, because thou canst not make one hair white or black. 37 But let your communication be, Yea, yea; Nay, nay: for whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil. - Matthew 5:33-37

You should never make oaths or swear to anything. When you are in court you should not swear to tell the truth and nothing but the truth. If you are a Christian you should always tell the truth, and be known for telling the truth. You should not swear because you cannot change anything by it. Jesus said you cannot make one hair black or white. If you cannot change even the color of your hair by swearing, then you could not even change things that really matter. I think it is just a waste of your good breath to swear.

You should just let your speech be simple. Jesus said that you should just let your yes be yes and your no be no. You should not use any words that are stronger than these. Many people today though swear all the time, and are around others doing the same too, so they are used to it. It is their normal everyday language. But among God's people this should not be so.

You need to start listening to yourself and counting the bad words you say, and then try cutting down on the amount of bad words you use each day. Your final goal will be to eliminate them altogether.

More Than The Law - Part 4: An eye for an eye

Original title: "Turn The Other Cheek"

Ye have heard that it hath been said, An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth: 39 But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other also. 40 And if any man will sue thee at the law, and take away thy coat, let him have thy cloak also. 41 And whosoever shall compel thee to go a mile, go with him twain. 42 Give to him that asketh thee, and from him that would borrow of thee turn not thou away. - Matthew 5:38-42

Telling someone to slap you on the other cheek sounds like a dumb thing to do. It is a dumb thing too, if you are not a Christian, that's because all humans have the sinful get-back-at nature. When you become a Christian you should start living God's way, his ways are higher than our ways. If someone was to slap you on the cheek, you should turn to him and say, "slap the other one". That person would probably not slap you again. If you would have done things your way you might slap him back, and then he would slap you back, and it would go on and on getting worse as it went.

If anyone takes away your tunic or coat, you should not get mad or blow-up about it, because you cannot change anything by getting mad. You should also give him your cloak. If you do things God's way he might just give your stuff back. As a Christian you should give to them that ask and turn not away him that would like to borrow from you. You, if you are a Christian, should know that all you have was given to you by God. The very God that gave you what you have can restore to you what you gave away and give away. (What will all the things you own profit you if you might lose your own soul? Matthew 16:26)

For as the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways, and my thoughts than your thoughts. - Isaiah 55:9

More Than The Law - Part 5: Thou Shalt Love thy Neighbour

Original title: "Love Your Enemies".

Ye have heard that it hath been said, Thou shalt love thy neighbour, and hate thine enemy. 44 But I say unto you, Love your enemies, bless them that curse you, do good to them that hate you, and pray for them which despitefully use you, and persecute you; 45 That ye may be the children of your Father which is in heaven: for he maketh his sun to rise on the evil and on the good, and sendeth rain on the just and on the unjust. 46 For if ye love them which love you, what reward have ye? do not even the publicans the same? 47 And if ye salute your brethren only, what do ye more than others? do not even the publicans so? 48 Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect. - Matthew 5:43-48

The first thing I want to do today is ask the question "why?”. Why in the world should I love my enemies? Why should I bless those that curse me? Why should I do good to those that hate me? Why should I pray for them which persecute me? O God why? There are many reasons why. The first reason is because Jesus Christ said to and He has authority. He said "you have heard that it has been said love your neighbors and hate your enemies, but I command you to love your enemies.” Another is so you can be a child of God. He loves all and if you want to be his child you must walk the way he walked and love the way He loves, love all. He makes his sun to shine on all and sends rain for those that hate Him and those that love Him.

You should also love your enemies because of the reward you get when you die. If you just love those that love you what do you more than others, what makes you think you earned a reward. You must love everyone even your enemies to have the reward of eternal life. If you just do what the publicans do then you will have the same reward as them eternal death.

If you are a Christian you are more and should do more than the average person. You should not just salute you friends, because that is what everyone does. You need to do more than other people. You need to salute and be friendly to everyone even your enemies.

The last but not least of the reasons is so you can be perfect like your father which is in heaven. No man can ever be completely perfect, but every man can start by loving his enemies, blessing them that curse him, doing good to them that hate him and praying for them which persecute him.

We love him, because he first loved us.

How to do Good

Take heed that ye do not your alms before men, to be seen of them: otherwise ye have no reward of your Father which is in heaven. 2 Therefore when thou doest thine alms, do not sound a trumpet before thee, as the hypocrites do in the synagogues and in the streets, that they may have glory of men. Today's Bible reading tells you how you should do your alms. The word alms mean charitable deeds or good deeds. Good deeds are like giving money, food, or clothes to the poor, or just being kind to others.

You need to make sure that when you do good you are not also being bad at the same time. There are a couple ways to be bad at the same time you are doing good. One way to do this is if you are doing it for the wrong reason and all you want is people to look at you and give you all the glory. You need to make sure you do the right thing for the right reason. Alms should be done in secret. Jesus said that you should not even let one hand know what the other hand is doing. I think that is getting a little carried away, but you should try to make sure that you are as secretive as possible. You should try to only let the person you are being good to know that you are doing good.

And whatsoever ye do, do it heartily, as to the Lord, and not unto men.
- Colossians 3:23

How to Fast

Moreover when ye fast, be not, as the hypocrites, of a sad countenance: for they disfigure their faces, that they may appear unto men to fast. Verily I say unto you, They have their reward. 17 But thou, when thou fastest, anoint thine head, and wash thy face; 18 That thou appear not unto men to fast, but unto thy Father which is in secret: and thy Father, which seeth in secret, shall reward thee openly. - Matthew 6:16-18

Fasting is something that people often don't talk about or do now days. Jesus must have wanted people to fast though, because he told us how. He said that you should not disfigure your face or appear to be sad. He said to wash your faces and be happy when you fast.
What is a fast?
A fast is like a prolonged quiet time. It is a time when you do not eat any food. A fast is a time when you do not let anything distract you. It is a time to pray, ask for forgiveness, meditate in God's word, and much more. Fasting is a thing between you and God, so you should not fast to be seen of men.

How long and often to fast?
It was tradition to fast about twice a week. You may not be able to fast that often, so just fast as often as you can. You should fast for about a whole day when you fast, but there is no limit to the amount of time you fast. Jesus fasted for 40 days one time.

How important it is to fast?
There are various reasons for fasting. The one main reason is because God commands us to. You should also fast to grow in your faith and knowledge of God. People also fast when they are going through hard times and they need to get back with God. All these reasons to fast make it important.

But as for me, when they were sick, my clothing was sackcloth: I humbled my soul with fasting; and my prayer returned into mine own bosom.
- Psalm 35:13

Is not the life more than meat, and the body than raiment? 26 Behold the fowls of the air: for they sow not, neither do they reap, nor gather into barns; yet your heavenly Father feedeth them. Are ye not much better than they? 27 Which of you by taking thought can add one cubit unto his stature? 28 And why take ye thought for raiment? Consider the lilies of the field, how they grow; they toil not, neither do they spin: 29 And yet I say unto you, That even Solomon in all his glory was not arrayed like one of these. 30 Wherefore, if God so clothe the grass of the field, which to day is, and to morrow is cast into the oven, shall he not much more clothe you, O ye of little faith? 31 Therefore take no thought, saying, What shall we eat? or, What shall we drink? or, Wherewithal shall we be clothed? 32 (For after all these things do the Gentiles seek:) for your heavenly Father knoweth that ye have need of all these things. 33 But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you. 34 Take therefore no thought for the morrow: for the morrow shall take thought for the things of itself. Sufficient unto the day is the evil thereof. - Matthew 6:19-34

If you are a Christian you need to have a different perspective on wealth than the unbeliever, a Christian perspective. Jesus said that you should not lay up treasures on the earth where moth and rust doth corrupt. You need to know that earthly riches are nothing, and lay up for yourself treasures in heaven. When you become a Christian you need to give your whole heart to God. If you have earthly treasures then your heart will not be set on things above.

Jesus talks about the eye being the light of the body right in the middle of talking about money. So I think he is using symbolism. He says that if even one of your eyes is evil your whole body will be full of darkness. I think what he means is that if one of your beliefs about money is wrong it will corrupt all your ideas about money.Your beliefs about money is like the foundation of a building, because money is the root of all evil.

He also talks about serving two masters, you will hate one or love one, but you can't love both. You cannot serve both God and riches. The way you live your life will show you who you serve.

If you are a Christian and want to serve God, you must live by his rules. You need to depend on Him and nothing else. Jesus said you should not even worry about what you are going to eat. God feeds even the birds and shall he not much more feed you, O you of little faith. Are you not much better than many sparrows? You are. You are priceless. You should not worry about your life because you cannot change anything by it, you need to take action and receive the things that God gives you.

Why take thought for clothes. If God clothes even the grass of the field shall he not also cloth you. He created the world and knew that you would need clothing. He even created the materials you need for making clothes, like cotton and wool.

You need to seek after God and his ways, and he shall bless you with all the other things you need. You need not think for your future or plan ahead to making it big, but you need to do all that you do to the Glory of God working with all your might. You should not worry for the morrow but meet each difficulty as it comes with God by your side. Sufficient unto each day is the evil thereof, do not make more evil by depending on your riches, instead of God.

They spend their days in wealth, and in a moment go down to the grave.

Judge Not

Judge not, that ye be not judged. 2 For with what judgment ye judge, ye shall be judged: and with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you again. 3 And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother's eye, but considerest not the beam that is in thine own eye? 4 Or how wilt thou say to thy brother, Let me pull out the mote out of thine eye; and, behold, a beam is in thine own eye? 5 Thou hypocrite, first cast out the beam out of thine own eye; and then shalt thou see clearly to cast out the mote out of thy brother's eye. 6 Give not that which is holy unto the dogs, neither cast ye your pearls before swine, lest they trample them under their feet, and turn again and rend you. - Matthew 7:1-6

In today’s Bible reading Jesus starts out with a command saying " Judge not". He also gives us one reason "that you be not judged". He is not saying that you will not be judged when you don't judge others.What he is saying is that when you judge others you will bring more judgment upon yourself. There are some things that are not bad and are actually good. If you judge others for doing those things and then do them yourself, you will be judged for it.

Jesus asks a question in the next verse, I think it is a silly question. He asks " why do you see the speck that is in your brother's eye, but not the plank that is in your eye". The answer to this question is easy God made us with eyes that see things. It seems that no matter how much you have blocking your eyesight you can always see others faults, because your eyes are always pointed away from you and not towards yourself. It is good that God made us this way and because we are made this way it means that we as Christians should work together to help others see their wrongs. We should also be willing to accept advice from others about what we need to fix in our lives.

The last verse in today's Bible reading says not to give that which is holy unto the dogs or to cast your pearls before swine. As a Christian your pearls should be the message of truth, you should share it with everyone, except the dogs or swine. The dogs and swine can be symbols of smart people that can trample you and hurt you. So what I think Jesus is saying is that you should not try to preach the gospel to people who can get the upper hand over you, and can trample you by proving you wrong. You need to study and learn more so that you can prove your faith by facts, then there shall be no more people that can trample you.

But why dost thou judge thy brother? or why dost thou set at naught thy brother? for we shall all stand before the judgment seat of Christ.
- Romans 14:10

Ask

Ask, and it shall be given you; seek, and ye shall find; knock, and it shall be opened unto you: 8 For every one that asketh receiveth; and he that seeketh findeth; and to him that knocketh it shall be opened. 9 Or what man is there of you, whom if his son ask bread, will he give him a stone? 10 Or if he ask a fish, will he give him a serpent? 11 If ye then, being evil, know how to give good gifts unto your children, how much more shall your Father which is in heaven give good things to them that ask him? - Matthew 7:7-11

In the first 2 verses it looks as if Jesus repeats himself, but He does not. In the first verse gives the command to, "ask", and in the second verse he gives a reason, "for every one that asks receives". If you ever want something all you have to do is ask.

Jesus uses some down to earth examples, and also says that if man, who is sinful, can give good gifts to them that ask how much more shall your Father which is in heaven give good things to them that ask Him.

Many people know that God knows our request before we ask him and wonder why we have to even ask him. The reason is because God wants us to depend on Him, if he just gave us things before we ask for them or know that we even have need of them then we would not have to depend on Him.

The Golden Rule

Therefore all things whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them: for this is the law and the prophets. - Matthew 7:12

The Golden rule, Do to others as you would have them do to you, applies to all areas of life and to all people. It helps people with their relationships and helps them to have a good relationship.

Let's say you went to a school and there was a bully there. If you treat him like a friend he will treat you like a friend, but if you treat him like he is a bully then he will treat you the way bullies treat people.

The golden rule also works in husband and wife relationships, but just because you want your wife to treat you like a husband does not mean that you should treat her like a husband.

The rule also works to help you have a wonderful relationship with God. You need to treat him like a Master or like a Loving Father or like a friend and he will treat you like His disciple or son or friend.
The Golden rule only works when you do your part, so make sure you do your part.

Philippians 2:4 Look not every man on his own things, but every man also on the things of others

Narrow Road To Heaven

Enter ye in at the strait gate: for wide is the gate, and broad is the way, that leadeth to destruction, and many there be which go in thereat: 14 Because strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be that find it. - Matthew 7:13-14

There are only two main ways to live your life by, and only two destinations. You can live your life any way you want but you are responsible for the turns and choices you make. The road to heaven is narrow, so if you want to get there you will have to travel on that road. You will have to do what I call "narrowing up" to be able to fit on that path. You will have to let go of the things that you cling to, like your money, good job, or any other earthly treasures. With those treasures your arms would be full and you would be too fat to fit on the Narrow road. It is hard to stay on that road because there are distractions. You will have to throw more things away from you so that you will be skinnier and then you will be able to get and hold weapons, to protect yourself from evil. Weapons include prayer and the Bible, your double edged sword. I like to imagine that the road to hell is like a huge circle. You can go in just about any and every direction and still be on the road to hell. You can even be good and still be on the road to hell.

How to get on the road you want .
Well to start off with you are born on the road to hell. So if you want to get on the right road you need to get born again. If you are born again you will know it and should know whether you are going to heaven or not. You need to remember though that just because you are traveling on one road, does not mean that you cannot get on another road, you need to make sure that you are always making the right turns(right choices).

EveryThing Is Known By It's Fruit

Beware of false prophets, which come to you in sheep's clothing, but inwardly they are ravening wolves. 16 Ye shall know them by their fruits. Do men gather grapes of thorns, or figs of thistles? 17 Even so every good tree bringeth forth good fruit; but a corrupt tree bringeth forth evil fruit. 18 A good tree cannot bring forth evil fruit, neither can a corrupt tree bring forth good fruit. 19 Every tree that bringeth not forth good fruit is hewn down, and cast into the fire. 20 Wherefore by their fruits ye shall know them. - Matthew 7:15-20

Jesus gives us a warning against false prophets today. He tells us how we will be able to know them so that we can stay away from them. He compares humans to trees and says that all you have to do is look at them and see what kind of fruit they produce. The false prophets will produce bad fruit and the good people will produce good fruit. If you are truly a Christian you will produce the fruit of the spirit which is love, joy, peace, long suffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, and temperance. Many people who become Christians still appear to be producing bad fruit. There are few reasons for this. One because they may not truly be Christians. Another is that no one is perfect. Apple trees produce good fruit but sometimes the fruit it produces has defects.

We have a plum tree at our house, every spring it looks nice with lots of flowers, but it never produces any fruit. We do not like that tree and plan to cut it down and burn it. Jesus says that is what will happen to people if they do not produce good fruit. So if you are a Christian you better produce fruit.

Conclusion: More Than Astonisted

And it came to pass, when Jesus had ended these sayings, the people were astonished at his doctrine: 29 For he taught them as one having authority, and not as the scribes. - Matthew 7:28-28

When Jesus first preached this message the people were astonished at his doctrine. I hope that you are more than astonished, I hope you will go out and start living your life by the sermon. My chief aim in writing this book was to bring glory to God's name. I was wondering how I could do that by just writing a book that talks about the Sermon on the Mount. But it can bring glory to His name if the people who read this book start living by the teachings of the Sermon on the Mount. You may wonder how you can live your life by it or what there is to live your life by in this sermon. So I am going to simplify the whole sermon to show you really how much there is in it that you should do.

In the Sermon on the Mount Jesus said that....
.....You should be a light for Jesus and shine your light everywhere.
....You need to keep every command because none have been done away with and you should teach them to others.
..... You must make your righteousness exceed the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees.
......You cannot kill or even say the word fool.
If you can do all that you're doing well but there is still more.
.....You should not commit adultery or divorce.
.... You should not swear.
.... You must always turn the other cheek.
....You must love, bless, and pray for your enemies.
.....You should never do your alms or prayers before people, but you should do a lot of both.
..... You should fast and be happy about it.
.... You should lay up for yourself treasures in heaven rather than on this earth.
.... You can never judge, and should never cast your pearls before swine.
.... You must always treat others the way you want treated.
.....You will have to give up all you got just so you will be able to fit through the narrow "canyon".
...You will have to produce fruit and do the will of God.

If you can do all that, you are doing good, but if you want to be blessed you will have to do all this and a lot more. Jesus also said that whosoever heareth these sayings of his and doeth them will be likened onto a man which builds his house on a rock. It is a lot to do all at once so I would recommend taking it one step at a time.
