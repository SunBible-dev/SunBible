(Welcome everyone with exuberance and excitement.
Sarah or I introduce myself and my family, and the inspiration behind this meeting.)

Thank you all so much for coming out on this cool afternoon to hear this message I've put together.

It was about 2 months ago now when I was praying one day that the idea first came to me about bringing Jesus to the park and having this gospel meeting, and at the time I didn't have any message ready.

And I was like, “yes, Lord this is good, I want to do this, but what do I speak on and who will come out to a park in the cold to hear a gospel message. Maybe in other countries, but here? Seriously? Here in America who wants to go out get cold and get uncomfortable for the Gospel's sake.

And as I thought of the Christians in different countries that are persecuted for their faith and will sometimes travel long distances sometimes just by foot, through the dark and cold, or rain or sleet to get to a secluded hidden area to read the Bible, sing and worship together and regardless of the danger is doing so.

So this is my theme for our meeting tonight. We have a lot to be thankful for here, we are not being persecuted and we all arrived in our warm vehicles and have hot chocolate to boot so we are pretty well off.

Let me say a quick prayer for us before I get started.

(Googling this more for my message I found varying articles, one said there are 232 countries, and another site 50 countries that Christianity and evangelism are illegal.

In some of these persecuted countries believers not only have to meet in secret but are brutally attacked and beaten for their faith.

And many of these people do not have the privilege of having a bible of their own either.

I've heard they have just copied pages of the Bible which are both well cherished, well-read.

Our persecuted brothers and sisters know that the Bible is both very precious and powerful.

They willingly risk their lives for their faith and once they come to know and love God they are not satisfied in just having faith to themselves.

But many, putting their own lives in great danger, diligently spread the gospel and tell others of their newfound Lord and savior!

I think the persecuted brothers and sisters have something on us that many of us don’t have. What is it that drives them to meet together in harsh and hostile environments, and to keep spreading the gospel regardless of the danger it puts them in?

For a long time, I certainly didn't have that passionate fire such as I've read in stories or articles of these people like in Voice Of Martyrs magazine. Oh, I was raised in a Christian home knowing God all my life and gave my heart to Him as a child and was baptized at 14. But for far too long I was satisfied with just sitting on my faith and hiding my little light, and I do mean little, under a bushel. ( you know the song this little light of mine )

But more recently I've become passionate about sharing my faith and hope that I have in Jesus with others, and the power of His word because sadly even many that are called by His name are still walking in bondage to the things of this World and not walking in the power and freedom God offers. It’s time to be set free and truly walk in the power of God! It's time to come alive and truly live! ( not like dead sheep )

Are you willing to take the steps it requires to have salvation in Christ and the hope of heaven?

First step: You must know that all have sinned

We have all sinned and therefore fall short of God's standard.

"As it is written , There is none righteous, no, not one." - Romans 3:10

"For all have sinned , and come short of the glory of God". - Romans 3:23

"Wherefore , as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned". - Romans 5:12

Second step: You must know that there is a penalty for sin

If we remain in our sin, we have the condemnation of death upon us, but if we repent and accept Jesus Christ as our Saviour, we receive eternal life.

"For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." - Romans 6:23

Third step: You must know that Christ died for (YOU)

Even though we are sinners, God loves us (you) and sent His beloved Son, the Lord Jesus Christ, to die in our (your) place and take the punishment of our sin.

"But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us." - Romans 5:8

Fourth step: You must call upon the Lord

We must call upon the Lord. God promises that when we call upon the name of the Lord we will be saved. There is no other name through which we can receive salvation.

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved." - Romans 10:13

Fifth step: You must believe and confess

We must believe in our hearts that Jesus died and shed his spotless blood, was buried, and rose again for our sins (1 Corinthians 15:1-4), but we must also confess out loud with our mouth that he is lord and savior.

"That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved. For with the heart man believeth unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation." - Romans 10: 9-10

Sixth step: You must know that it is a faith walk

We receive God's salvation by faith. When we are saved, we keep walking by faith, which grows stronger as we read His word and experience His power.

"Faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God." - Romans 10:17

If you have never made Jesus Christ the king of your life and if you have never been born again or if your children are not yet born again, here is a prayer to pray.

" Dear Father, I know that I have sinned and deserve the punishment of my sin, which is death. But I thank you that you have died in my place, and took my sin upon yourself as you died upon the cross and poured out your blood. I confess aloud right now: JESUS CHRIST IS THE LORD AND SAVIOR OF MY LIFE. amen"
