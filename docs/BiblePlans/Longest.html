How to Cleanse Your way

Psalm 119:9-16 starts with a question. A very important question I might add. It is a question that many struggle with. The question is “How shall we cleanse Our way?”. We all need an answer to this question because we are all sinners who need our way cleaned. Luckily for us Psalm 119:9 gives us the answer: “By taking heed to God's word”.

You may be wondering “how is that a way to cleanse my way?”. Well God's word is filled with laws and commands and by taking heed to them your way will be cleansed. God's word is also filled with encouragement to help you cleanse your way.

Conclusion

We are both just about done with this 22 day study of Psalm 119. I think we have both learned a lot. You may be thinking that you can now walk away from this book and continue life as you used to live it. But you can't. If you have learned things, then you need to start acting upon them. It won't be easy to adjust your life, but you can take the first step by saying “I will”.
Say “I will...
…See Verse List (Notice how many are from Psalm 119)
Saying “I will” is only a first small easy step. The second step, doing it, is much harder and will take much more time. Really if you are a Christian your whole life should be dedicated to doing the will of God. Many people think they have to become missionaries to serve God and so do not even try to serve God or do His will because they think they have to go all the way around the world to do it. But you can be a missionary right there in your home, at your job site, and/or anywhere else you happen to be. Plus you should live your life in such a way that when your end is near you will be able to say “I have”.

Able to say “I have” like David: “I have kept Thy precepts and Thy testimonies: for all my ways are before Thee.” Able to say “l have” like Paul: "I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the faith" Or you may have to end up saying: “I have gone astray like a lost sheep; seek thy servant; for I do not forget thy commandments.” But hopefully you won't have to end up saying that. The truth is we all make mistakes, and when we do we need to be humble enough to confess it to God.
